On Fri, Dec 9, 2011 at 8:48 AM, Chris <kc2rgw at gmail.com> wrote:

> I prefer msg traffic to run over NBEMS, but packet can do things that
> a one-way msg form cannot.  Until NBEMS can "Find where John Smith is
> located?" we will also need to keep packet around.
>

What this points out is that we need a better system that is far more
automated. Surprisingly, there are very few things that can be done with a
packet-based system that cannot be done with a file-transfer system given
you can accept a little bit of latency. I refer you to the uucp system if
you don't believe what I say. Most unix users forget just how well uucp
actually worked as long as you weren't looking for instant feedback, e.g.
command-line request/response.

So, I see the right answer as rolling out better protocols.
