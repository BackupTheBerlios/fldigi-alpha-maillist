3.13BP is working great here so far.

And I see the ability to select/de-select the RSID for the various modes 
and the ability to select "opmode" modes view.

These changes are fantastic and will be a great help for our emcomm ops 
here in WPA.  We're going to be encouraging folks (our team members and 
EMA/EOC stations) to monitor our call frequency on 80m.  RSID select 
will allow them to park on a fixed freq, and set RSID to only detect 
Olivia or MT63 (our primary wrap modes).  We use Olivia 8/500 for short 
wrap messages and MT63 for detailed reports, spread sheets and blocks of 
formal messages as separate wraps in a single transmission.

An EOC, for instance, would only have to turn on the HF rig to our freq 
in a memory channel, turn on the computer with all the settings already 
configured, and all they would need to know is how to check the WRAP 
folder as any formal, priority or emergency traffic which we would 
always send as wrap.  This acts as a kind of filter so they wouldn't 
have to sift through chat and diddle to see the important traffic.

BTW, I am not advocating sending messages to specific 
unmanned/unlicensed stations which would be a violation of the 
regulations.  However, there is nothing saying that an unlicensed 
station is prohibited from monitoring traffic that we send between our 
licensed stations.  The ultimate goal is to show the value in digi 
emcomm and put some of the gear to use at EOCs and EMAs which would give 
us a better chance of being invited in to run the stations during drills 
and actual emergencies.

Thanks again to the team for all the great work!  We're putting it to 
good use here in WPA!

-Dave, KB3FXI
