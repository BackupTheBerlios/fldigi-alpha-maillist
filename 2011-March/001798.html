I do not think the flipping of the VFO's has anything to do with RigCAT or 
HamLib. It seems to be an fldigi issue between fldigi-3.21.4 and fldigi-3.22.0AA.
I just tired it again and fldigi-3.21.4 commands the TS-590S VFO-A, but does 
not communicate at all with VFO-B.  

I quit fldigi-3.21.4 and launched 3.22.0AA with VF0-A still active on the
Kenwood display and there was no communications with VFO-A. The QSY 
button changes fldigi on the waterfall, but does not change the TS-590S. 
I then depressed the A/B button on the TS-570S to make VFO-B active and
fldigi-3.22.0AA commands the rig and the QSY button functions normal as
long as VF0-B is active.

I am posting this response to the alpha list to see if this weirdness shows
up in other setups.  I cannot explain the flipping of VFO's between two
versions of fldigi.

The inability of fldigi to detect and command the active VFO is a limitation
to fldigi/Kenwood TS-590S users. Hopefully, this shortfall can be corrected 
in the future.
