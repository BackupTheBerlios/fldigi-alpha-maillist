On 11/8/2010 15:32, Brian Lloyd wrote:
  > Why would it go via CW?

Because that's what the nets are that gives me *some* chance
of actually getting the message close to where it's going.
I have maybe 6 stations in the entire state of Colorado that
I can get messages to and I'm the only one that is interested
in the digital modes of the ones that will take traffic.
We do have a pretty active state ARES group that regularly
practices digital modes but they are not the least interested
in taking routine messages in the ARRL format.  Additionally,
the mode of choice is Pactor - 1, 2 and 3.

End delivery has always been the hardest part of message
handling.  It does no good to have a modern, fast and error
free method of moving traffic across the country if there
is no one on the end to deliver the message.  Just the way
it is.

  Why not preserve the message and its wrapper
> end-to-end? A wrapped message may be sent using a more robust and
> error-free mechanism that will operate with a lower carrier-to-noise
> ratio than CW. In addition CW introduces a much greater chance for error.
