The differences in opinion seems to be the interpretation of NIMS
compliance.  When it comes to forms there is the thought that as long as the
format is used (layout) then some flexibility in column headers should be
allowed or as is usually stated "Agency Need Specific".

The whole idea of NIMS is to provide a common framework for
interoperability. Some folks are still struggling with that concept.

As a COMM L I always have to determine the set of forms the Incident
Commander has chosen and become aware of differences.
The 205 is usually generated within COMMS so we generate the form from the
outset.

2 of the 3 tool kit 205's I have and the FEMA form 205 are identical.  The
3rd 205 is an agency specific form/format.

I feel we would always be better positioned if we use the FEMA forms.  The
only problem so far is when you look at their website and
download a form it may not have a date of implementation on it.  The 205 pdf
from the FEMA website is a good example.  This makes it difficult to keep up
with new changes.  FEMA could do a better job (adopt some best practices
from other agencies) in this area.

All forms should have a date to determine the latest version to be used.

I have looked at all 205's from previous exercises and deployments we have
been involved in and all are of the FEMA format.

You are correct regarding Standardization.  That is the whole purpose of the
forms and the NIMS concept.

We would be safe using the current FEMA forms.

We will have to make sure we keep up with any future changes for forms we
use.

Thanks,

Mike

> Somehow I think the concept of standardization has been lost in this
> discussion. An ICS 213 Form is an ICS 213 Form. I don't think it is perfect
> for every application, but if you decide that it needs to be changed to work
> in your environment then you have created a new form. Your modified form is
> not an ICS 213 Form, and you should not call it one.
>
> I recommend that if additional information is required to process the
> message, then an additional routing form or disposition form be attached as
> required. I suggest that failure to maintain the expected format might delay
> the transmission of the message and "might actually hinder an effective
> response to an incident". Simple items like Incident Name and Message Number
> can be incorporated into the existing fields by procedure.
