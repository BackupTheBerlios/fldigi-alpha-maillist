During a QPSK QSO with G3PLX the originator of PSK31 today, he pointed
out that my signal did not include about a second of centre-frequency CW
at the end of transmission, with the result that his demod was
displaying some garbage characters at the end of my transmission. The CW
"postamble" tells the demod that the transmission has finished and to
tidy up cleanly.

The fldigi demod appears to work correctly: if the centre-frequency
postamble is received no garbage characters are displayed, but if the
transmission is abruptly stopped while in the two-tone idle state random
characters are shown, as expected.

Checking the fldigi QPSK output on a monitor receiver here confirms that
a two-tone rather than single-tone postamble is sent for all QPSK variants.

BPSK is fine and sends the single-tone postamble as expected.
