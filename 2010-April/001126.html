Brian,

We again tested DominoEx against Contestia on UHF today with the 
customary Doppler disturbance and DominoEx 8 did poorly, but Contestia 
had perfect copy. Signals were S3, so moderately strong. This is not the 
first time Contestia has outperformed DominoEx by a wide margin. In this 
screen capture, the "Doppler kink" in the spurious seen in the DominoEx 
waterfall is not there in Contestia, but was there a minute earlier and 
Contestia printed perfectly the entire time. You can tell from the text 
that DominoEx 8 was producing poor copy, but signals were fairly strong.

> Contestia and Olivia are virtually identical CODECs. Their performance 
> is going to be within one or two dB of each other so what you expect 
> from Olivia you can expect from Contestia. 
>
> Contestia uses a small character set so it needs fewer bits 
> transferred for the same message. This also means that you don't get 
> lower case letters and many punctuation symbols you can send with 
> Olivia. Also Contestia has a smaller block size and shorter 
> interleave. This means less delay but less resistance to long fading 
> (QSB). If you increase the interleave to provide QSB resistance you 
> can expect longer delays (latency) before copy starts.
>
> In this same family of protocols you will find MFSK16, DominoEX, and 
> Thor. Olivia, Contestia, and MFSK16 all share the same base modulation 
> -- m-ary frequency shift keying. The differences are in the 
> alphabet/character-set they use, the symbol (baud) rate, and the 
> robustness of the forward error correction (FEC). If you set the 
> #tones (always a power of 2, e.g. 2, 4, 8, 16, 32), symbol rate 
> (determines tone spacing), and level of FEC the same on these 
> protocols, they will provide the same speed and weak-signal performance.
>
> (Soapbox -- We would be better served by having just one of these 
> modes and then allow selection of the tones, baud rate, convolution 
> polynomial, block size, interleave factor, and Viterbi decoder 
> settings and then transmit that in something equivalent to an RSID 
> header.)
>
> DominoEX and Thor use a novel and, frankly, better modulation scheme 
> than the pure MFSK used in MFSK16/Olivia/Contestia. All other things 
> being equal, if you were to put DominoEX with FEC or Thor up against 
> on the three other above but ensure that you are using like parameters 
> for baud rate, convolutional coding, and FEC, DominoEX and Thor would 
> be easier to tune and more resilient in the presence of doppler. In 
> other words, they would flat-out work better.
>
> I apologize for what is a really long answer to your question. I hope 
> it helps to understand the differences and to set reasonable 
> expectations for performance. 
>
> Hmm, it would be great if there were a table that listed the basic 
> parameter differences between modes so we could pick the one that best 
> met conditions at the time so as to provide maximum throughput at 100% 
> copy. Frankly, most of the time Olivia is overkill but it also 
> provides the most robust FEC and interleave so it will get through 
> under the worst conditions when the other modes are failing.
