Mike,

1. I suspect it is trying to access an "unvalid" portaudio device. You can
try to delete the .fldigi directory under your home directory. In that case
I believe Fldigi will try for the OSS devices (/dev/dsp) first, then you can
try to re-selct the desired portaaudio device.

2. The GNU library (GLIBC) is at the core of your operating system and I
think it is pretty difficult to upgrade it independantly. Hence the benefit
of re-compiling with your version of GLIBC.

Hope this helps

Regards, John
