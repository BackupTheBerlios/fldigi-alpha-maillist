I believe you are seeing the result of fft processing in fldigi.  
Because of the interaction between the fft block sampling rate and the 
signal baud rate you may see a smear of the waterfall in a downward 
direction.  You can reduce this by setting the waterfall "fft latency" 
to 1 and disable FFT averaging.

I have posted wav files for you to playback and also Audacity analyses 
of these waveforms that clearly show that the precursor tones and the 
data tones are concurrent.

http://www.w1hkj.com/spectrum_tests/4-500.wav
http://www.w1hkj.com/spectrum_tests/4-500-tones.csv
http://www.w1hkj.com/spectrum_tests/4-500-data.csv

http://www.w1hkj.com/spectrum_tests/8-250.wav
http://www.w1hkj.com/spectrum_tests/8-250-tones.csv
http://www.w1hkj.com/spectrum_tests/8-250-data.csv

Each wav file includes both a video and RSID preamble.  You should be 
able to set RxID ON and automatically lock to the playback waveforms.  
Properly locked the s/n should be 29 dB and the f/o = 0.0 Hz.

The *tones.csv file are tab delimited files that you can open in Excel, 
Gnumeric, or Open Office.  Try plotting the spectrum and observing the 
tone placement.

Ditto for the *data.csv which is an Audacity spectral analysis of the 
data portion only.

I hope this explains what you are seeing.  If it something other than 
what I describe and the files indicate then I need substantive data 
capture to allow me to analyze the problem.  Ie:  Rx waveform capture 
when you observe the problem.  I can provide you with an upload url for 
large files.
