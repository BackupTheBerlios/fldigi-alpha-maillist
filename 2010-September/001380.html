On Tue, Sep 21, 2010 at 7:57 AM, Charles Brabham <n5pvl at uspacket.org> wrote:

> Q15x25 mode uses fifteen PSK streams to return an effective 2500 baud data
> rate - if all goes well. In tests on HF, Q15x25 was not very robust at all.
> It would work well with a strong, clean link but under typical HF conditions
> it slowed down considerably. In some conditions, HF Packet at 300 baud
> provided better throughput than Q15x25 mode at 2500 baud.
>
> As in the story of the tortoise and the hare, an emphasis on getting
> through can be much more productive than concentrating all effort upon
> simply going faster... Sometimes less is more.
>

The problem is, HF propagation through the ionosphere is NOT a
gaussian-noise-limited channel. Fast PSK does NOT usually fare well over
HF.

FWIW it would be good for LoS VHF/UHF communications.

If you are interested in fast datacomm over HF you need to study what has
been accomplished with PACTOR and WinMOR.
