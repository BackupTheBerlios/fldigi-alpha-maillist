As long as two different installations of fldigi interpret the non-standard 
settings the same, there should be no technical reason.  Here in the USA, 
there would be some limitations regarding baud rate on HF.  For example, I 
believe that PSK-500 is illegal on HF because of the limit of  300 baud PER 
CARRIER.  Higher bands have a limit of 1200 baud, so PSK-1000 woud be about 
as high as you could go.

You could get around those lmits by usng a mode which divides the data among 
multiple carriers.  I don't know if Cuba imposes any baud rate limits.

PSK-3000 might pose some problems since many transceivers might not be able 
to handle a 3 Khz wide signal.
