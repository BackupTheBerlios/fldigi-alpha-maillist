With all the wonderful things Mac OSX brings us there are still some things
it doesn't do as well as other OS's.

Regardless of OS being used, I learned early on as an Alpha tester to be
prepared to delete or rename the current config file especially if one
encounters strange behaviors not previously seen.

It can be inconvenient at times but if we are to contribute to the
successful growth of the various programs and contibute to furthering the
art and science of Amateur Radio in this way it comes with the territory.

I do most all my testing on the Macbook and leave my Mini working stable
releases.

One approach for Mac testers could be to set up another account on the Mac
that is used for testing only and keep the main account for stable releases.

Just a thought.
