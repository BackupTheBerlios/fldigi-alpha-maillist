>> Jim,
>>
>> Here is how I do it.  First I go back to LOTW and press the "Most 
>> Recent QSOs" button near the bottom, which give me a list of what 
>> they already have, and I take the last date and call sign for my 
>> starting point for the next upload.  Now that I know where I want to 
>> start, I go over to FLDIGI and open the  Logbook-ADIF-Export  and 
>> select the fields TQSL wants to see, the most important ones being 
>> the date and time on.  Then go over to the log and click on each of 
>> the check boxes for QSO's you want to send to LOTW.  Then click OK, 
>> Name it with a unique name, (I use sequential numbering i.e. 
>> W6HGF1234.adi), and run it through TQSL and upload to LOTW.
>>
>>   For Dave,  I kind of like the XLOG method of numbering the QSO's 
>> and letting me specify the qso number range for the extraction 
>> process, it is much, much easier than all the little check boxes 
>> after a long contest, and I have never run across an occasion yet 
>> where I needed to skip a qso in the middle.
>>
>>   Also a note, I really miss the auto fill 599 on the log, something 
>> I got used to and now sometimes miss and have to go back and fix 
>> before submissions.

Dave,

   Here is an example from this weekend 160 contest.  2 QSO's from 
FLDIGI with all the default boxes checked comes out in adif like this.
File: export.adif
<ADIF_VER:5>2.2.3
<PROGRAMID:6>fldigi
<PROGRAMVERSION:8>3.21.0BE
<EOH>
<CALL:5>W0ANT<FREQ:8>1.824918<MODE:2>CW<QSO_DATE:8>20110130<QSO_DATE_OFF:8>20110130<TIME_OFF:4>0547<TIME_ON:4>0547<EOR>
<CALL:5>K6SRZ<FREQ:8>1.827190<MODE:2>CW<NAME:4>Alan<QSO_DATE:8>20110130<QSO_DATE_OFF:8>20110130<RST_RCVD:3>599<RST_SENT:3>599<TIME_OFF:4>0611<TIME_ON:4>0611<EOR>

When run through TQSL which is the LOTW edit and signing/encryption 
program, they format the same data like this.
<CALL:5>W0ANT
<BAND:4>160M
<MODE:2>CW
<QSO_DATE:8>20110130
<TIME_ON:6>054700
<FREQ:8>1.824918
<EOR>
<CALL:5>K6SRZ
<BAND:4>160M
<MODE:2>CW
<QSO_DATE:8>20110130
<TIME_ON:6>061100
<FREQ:8>1.827190
<EOR>

So you can see much of the data is not used by LOTW, that we like to 
keep in our logbooks for future reference or paper qsl's.  Also one 
difference worth noting in naming conventions, LOTW uses the suffix 
.adi  instead of .adif     There was a great write-up about LOTW in this 
months QST on the benefits of the system.  You might be surprised how 
many new awards like triple-play you have already qualified for, once 
you upload your logbook to LOTW.   One more important reason I use the 
system, is that I have thought about future researchers and even current 
ones, what fun it would be to have the original W6AM logs, or W1AW logs 
from the turn of the century to examine a hundred years from now.  LOTW 
should become a legacy archive for those of us that come and go, that 
can be there for our great grand children to discover someday, and enjoy 
a part of our hobby sometime in the future, the way we do today.

Al  W6HGF
