Jim,

Here is how I do it.  First I go back to LOTW and press the "Most Recent 
QSOs" button near the bottom, which give me a list of what they already 
have, and I take the last date and call sign for my starting point for 
the next upload.  Now that I know where I want to start, I go over to 
FLDIGI and open the  Logbook-ADIF-Export  and select the fields TQSL 
wants to see, the most important ones being the date and time on.  Then 
go over to the log and click on each of the check boxes for QSO's you 
want to send to LOTW.  Then click OK, Name it with a unique name, (I use 
sequential numbering i.e. W6HGF1234.adi), and run it through TQSL and 
upload to LOTW.

   For Dave,  I kind of like the XLOG method of numbering the QSO's and 
letting me specify the qso number range for the extraction process, it 
is much, much easier than all the little check boxes after a long 
contest, and I have never run across an occasion yet where I needed to 
skip a qso in the middle.

   Also a note, I really miss the auto fill 599 on the log, something I 
got used to and now sometimes miss and have to go back and fix before 
submissions.
