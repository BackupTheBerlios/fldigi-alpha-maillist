Dick,

These are all within 4 dB of each other and if you are adjusting the ALC for
one bar your settings are too tight. Lower the gain so that you have no ALC
action, then a bit farthe and you should not need to touch the TX attenuator
again.

> Some interesting observation of TX Atten settings by band with the
> TS-590S under Mac OS X 10.6.7.
> 
> I used the FB keyboard shortcuts to tweak in each setting in the PSK or RTTY
> portion of the bands.  I try to have no more that one bar flashing in in the
> ALC position on the TS-590's S-Meter. I know 'they say' zero ALC but when
> nothing shows on meter you may be too low.  This is why I tune display on
> bar flashing.
> 
>  7.035 MHz = -12.1
> 10.143 MHz = -13.8
> 14.085 MHz = -11.3
> 18.103 MHz = -12.2
> 21.070 MHz = -11.7
> 28.120 MHz = -12.0
> 50.300 MHz = -10.7
> 
> I really like the new addition and the keyboard shortcuts are useful and used.
> 
> I wonder what attenuation levels others are requiring to adjust out the ALC?
> 
> By using the new TX Atten tool I am now able to use the TS-590's default
> setting 
> of Step #4 for Input Data.  Before having the tool Step #2 was too low and
> Step #3 was too high and I had little on no wiggle room on OS X Sound/Output
> volume slider. It was near the stop on the left of side of the slider. It is
> now
> sitting at about 25% with good wiggle room.
