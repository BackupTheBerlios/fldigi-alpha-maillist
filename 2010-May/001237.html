Guys,

I OS-X there is another way that you can get to the normally hidden
~/.fldigi folder: open terminal (which by default opens in $HOME. Link the
.fldigi to a visible name with the command "ln -s .fldigi dot-fldigi" .
The you can just click on dot-fldigi in the normal Finder window and you are
in the ~/.fldigi folder! This makes future navigation to the log and macros
folders much easier.

The other less obvious way is to use the GoTo Folder option in Finder.
