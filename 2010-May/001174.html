But....  as mentioned earlier in this thread.. the average person you
are training has no clue about digital modes and hence the fact that
they get stumped on the 3583 'thing".  This issue is not really FLdigi
related but it reveals the fundamental problem associated with
emcomms...  use by competent operators.  Twenty years ago, I was in a
air-plane crash simulated disaster and all the ambulance and police
communications failed because they jammed their frequencies and were
not adept enough to figure out what to do next.  My PK232 and small
Icom 2M rig adapted to the conditions and got all the traffic through.
 Twenty years later we have numerous emcomm hams that are being
trained in use of NBEMS but there is a danger that we turn them in to
appliance operators just like the ambulance drivers.  When NBEMS was
first introduced a few years back, Skip espoused a belief that NBEMS
had some advantages over other systems in that it it required no
special training and employed methods used by digital hams in their
everyday casual QSOs.  I will argue that while wanting to keep it
simple is a laudable idea, hams being trained should also be trained
on how to get frequency readout to work .

Andy K3UK
