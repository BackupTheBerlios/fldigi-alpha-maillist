Rick,

My original point is directed toward, in our case, the vast majority of 
ops who don't use, or have no intention of ever using, Rig control, 
which in my opinion is nothing but a distraction from what I thought was 
the intended purpose of NBEMS which is "Emergency" Communications, not 
flashy contesting/logging features like you see on HRD/DM780.

When someone downloads and installs FLDIGI for the first time, they see 
3850, which is just pointless and confusing. Why is it a major point of 
contention to suggest that you display zeros rather than show an 
arbitrary frequency when rig control is not being deployed?

Here's the text from the help file:
		###################################
Both are shown on Windows Vista controlling the author's FT-450.  The 
same control is used when the transceiver does not support CAT.  When no 
CAT is available the control is simply a convenient way of keeping track 
of the transceiver USB/LSB frequency, the mode and the audio tracking point.
		###################################

Perhaps it should also say, "3583 is just a random figure we decided to 
set as a default when rig control is not deployed and does not convey 
any relevant or useful information to the user".

-Dave
