On Sat, May 1, 2010 at 7:10 AM, Andy obrien <k3ukandy at gmail.com> wrote:

I will argue that while wanting to keep it simple is a laudable idea, hams
> being trained should also be trained on how to get frequency readout to work
> .
>

Why? What's the benefit? Odds are nearly 100% that the radio already reads
out the frequency if it is smart enough to be controllable by fldigi, and
that in turn means it doesn't need fldigi to duplicate the information on
the display. Further, as these are AFSK modes, the waterfall frequency
display is certainly appropriate in AF frequencies.

In an emergency, isn't the frequency display just so much fluff, duplicating
the functionality of the radio's own display? Shouldn't hams who are,
presumably, doing something important and perhaps even with lives depending
upon them, be concentrating on the things that actually facilitate the task,
rather than making sure a duplicated display is doing its duplication
properly? Since tuning to an AF frequency across the waterfall is the most
direct method for correct reception - these modes require specific AF
inputs, and in AF display, the waterfall is 100% usable for tuning and
doesn't need tweaking or interaction with the radio to be 100% correct -
isn't that the optimum way to use it, especially in an emergency situation?
