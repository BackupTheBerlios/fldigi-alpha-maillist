Dave,

Maybe the disconnect in this discussion has to do with the target audience.
I am working with three different groups of people.  The first and the most
fun are the digital geeks like me who enjoy playing with things like fldigi.
Having 3.580 displayed is only a mild curiosity and of not concern for
them.

The second group are the folks we are trying to train for emergency
operation during a disaster.  These are motivated folks who volunteer
for Red Cross operations, are volunteer firefighters, and volunteers for
the local Emergency Operations Center.  Those are the people we are
trying to help the most.  They are not interested in how or why fldigi,
wrap, or flics works.  They just want to know which buttons to push to
get their messages through.  Being able to remove all the unnecessary
stuff from the screen for them was a tremendous boost for that.

The third group is average hams who have a curiosity about digital
operation.  The problem with this group is that they are really the
folks who know essentially nothing about digital operation and don't
have the technical background to just download fldigi and fire it up.
Giving a demo at a club meeting can sometimes be a challenge.  They
start without a clue what to look for and any distraction like having a
frequency display as 3.580 and as offsets from there simply leads them
off into the weeds.  I have to agree, I have never demonstrated fldigi
and not been asked why that number is shown.

For the folks who manually enter a frequency, starting with zeros on the
initial load should not be any different than starting with 3.580.  Or am I
missing something here and everyone does actually operate on 80 meter
psk31 all the time  : -)

I dunno, just my spin on this subject.

Gary - N0GW
