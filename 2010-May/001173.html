Gary,

The point here is that Harry and I, here in WPA, are training TONS of 
people on NBEMS for use in EmComm.  We travel within (and occasionally 
beyond) the WPA ARES section to do demo's, training and occasional 
station setups.

The 3583 thing is obviously not that big of a deal, but it comes up as a 
question over and over again and neither the instructions or the 
explanations for it here make much sense to me or the people we're 
training. It just winds up being a waste of our time having to 
continually address this issue.

One thing we're getting ready to do after the next major release is to 
do a fresh install on the various platform versions and standardize all 
the settings and macros for our current and future volunteers. We will 
have the extended characters checked, long interleave for MT63, auto 
detection and extraction, RSID for only our selected modes of 
operations, etc. Then we'll copy and save the program files that hold 
this data and simply have our members copy our standardized files and 
overwrite the ones in their folders.  We'll have the CAT/Loggin display 
set to be hidden as it serves no useful purpose for the vast majority of 
our operations here in WPA.

-Dave, KB3FXI
