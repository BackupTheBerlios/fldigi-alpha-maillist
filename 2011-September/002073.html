>> > Yes, many fldig users set the sweet spot to 1500 Hz to center the Rx
signal to the "fixed" filters in their rigs for better decoding; however,
this may not be the best sweet spot for Tx.
>> >
>> > As a test use your fldigi "QSY" button to center your rig's VFO to
where you want to operate. The QSY button centers your Rx and Tx at the
defined sweet spot. Now transmit and monitor you output to see if you
transmitted signal sounds right. Now mouse to the left side of the
waterfall, but DO NOT use the QSY button. Transmit and listen to your
signal. Then do the same on right side of he waterfall. You will probably
notice a pitch change in the your Tx. When I was using 1500 Hz as the sweet
spot, my Tx signal sounded better to me when I used 1000 Hz as the sweet
spot. By sounding better I mean my transmitted RTTY signal sounded more like
what recalled as normal FSK signal. The same applies to PSK and CW
transmissions. For this reason I now use 1000 Hz as my sweet spot and I have
adjusted the "variable" TS-590's Rx filters to accordingly. When I engage
the The TS-590's filters, all signals outside the bandwidth are blanked.I
can change the bandwidth on-the-fly depending on congestion.
>> >
>> > If I see a signal I want to work on either side of the sweet spot on
the waterfall, I mouse on the signal, use the "QSY" button to move the
signal (and VFO) to the sweet spot. My Rx and Tx are now in sync using the
really cool "QSY" button W1HKJ incorporated into fldigi. For S&P operation I
use the "QSY" button to sync my Rx and Tx and manually tune with the VFO to
bring stations to the Space and Mark lines on the waterfall. If I see one I
want to work I use the QSY again to center the station, let the AFC take
over then call the station.
>> >

sounding signals. This probably means they are transmitting up or down the

>> > Like you it took me four revisions to get CQ Robot to accept my
Cabrillo format. The Robot nag notes will point out what may be wrong;
however, the comments are obscure and you have to fix the first problem
reported before it will let you move on to others. The first error was the
ACCESSED vs. NON-ACCESSED. I had used NON-ASSESSED but the robot did not
like the format. I did a copy/paste from nag note and that cleared that
issue. The show stopper was
>> >
>> > I do not recognize '' as a valid QTH.
>> > [X] QSO: 14112 RY 2011-09-24 0034 AA5VU 599 04 TX PZ5RA 599 09
>> > Line ignored.
>> >
>> > and the fix was to change the PZ5RA from "599 09" to "599 09 DX"
>> >
>> > This was caused by my interpretation of the rules saying the exchange
for Non US stations should be "RST + CQ Zone" so that was the way I setup
the macro to log the exchange. It never entered my mine to add a "0" in the
logged report for Non US stations. Susan, K5DU, sent me a tip to add the
"DX" in place of the state. Once the "DX" was added, the CQ Robot smiled and
accepted the aa5vu.log file.
