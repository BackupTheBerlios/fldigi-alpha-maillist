> The one
> consistent trend I've noticed is that the more "primitive" the sound
> subsystem the better it is in this application. Less versatile, maybe.

Not exactly. Soundcard makers of late have been getting lazy in supplying 
hardware capable of handling all of the usual sample rates natively. Instead 
they rely on the OS to do the conversion. 44100 is CD quality, but most higher 
end cards generally support (at least) 48000 which has lower overhead in 
conversions to some other sample rates. 

For radio purposes it is better to have a stable soundcard that can support 
all of the sample rates it needs to without requiring the OS to do a 
conversion, as all conversions are not necessarily as clean as handling the 
stream at the intended sample rate in the first place. That's why some sample 
rate conversion ratios require greater overhead and produce less satisfactory 
results.
