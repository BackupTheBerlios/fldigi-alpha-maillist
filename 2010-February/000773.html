* On 2010 01 Feb 11:48 -0600, Pino Zollo wrote:
> Hi all,
> 
> I propose an option for sending CW:
> 
> Instead of using the audio card, send characters to cwdaemon.

Somewhere in the Linuxham Yahoo Group archive I suggested the same
thing.  Dave's explanation was that above 35 WPM or so cwdaemon begins
to have misformed elements due to kernel latencies which can be
partially overcome by a "real time kernel".  Fldigi's use of the sound
card eliminates those problems and can be used for some very high
speeds.

Better, IMO, is my suggestion that Fldigi act as a replacement for
cwdaemon itself by being able to listen on port 6789 and processing the
cwdaemon commands but using its soundcard keying method.  This way all
past, current, and future Linux programs that utilize cwdaemon could
standardize on the cwdaemon feature set whether the real daemon is
provided by Fldigi, Winkeyer USB Server, or cwdaemon itself.

I'm encouraged that the Fldigi developers have not dismissed this idea
out of hand.  Yet.
