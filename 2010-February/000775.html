However, I don't think we should limit our software by our own
limitations.  If cwdaemon truly has problems sending at higher speeds,
and Dave's explanation makes sense to me, then is it possible that the
lack of high speed CW generation is holding back the development of
contest grade logging software?  It think it might be.  In fact the
K1EL Winkeyer and N0XAS MK-1 were developed to address the same issue
on MS Windows:

http://www.hamgadgets.com/store/product_info.php?products_id=106

I may only be able to copy above 25 WPM with difficulty and multiple
repeats, but I don't wish to limit those who can work those speeds and
faster.  As Fldigi can provide error-free computer generated Morse at
high speeds, I'd like to see other software be able to take advantage
of it.
