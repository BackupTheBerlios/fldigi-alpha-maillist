You are perhaps right about my comments on the specifics of PSKmail
but not about my comments on propagation in general. I have a great
deal of clue about the effects of HF propagation on various modulation
schemes.

> We have 5 years experience with this, and we would not be using PSK250 if it did not work...
> When it works it is 4x faster than any other mode.

And there is the operant phrase -- "when it works". Yes, PSK250 is
going to work under some conditions, e.g. single-hop propagation near
the MUF where multipath is nonexistent. Once you start to get
multipath the doppler phase modulates the signal greatly increasing
bit error rate.

> In those cases where  it does not work we are using THOR22 or MFSK32, depending on band condx.

That is very sensible because the modes based on MFSK, e.g. Thor,
DominoEX, MFSK16/32, etc, are designed with long integration times.
They are also noncoherent so phase modulation due to doppler is no
factor.

> Actually, we use PSK250, PSK125, THOR22, THOR11, THOR8, MFSK32, MFSK16.
> We can switch modes during a session. By using RSID on the servers (using fldigi-3.13)
> the mode is no issue anymore.... Still PSK250 Is used 90% of the time...

I understand. Thank you for clarifying. Next time I will do better

on my general experience that hams will generally come up with a
substandard way of doing things and then cling to that with a tenacity
suggesting religion.
